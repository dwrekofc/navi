# Repair Local Sync Database

If you’re seeing the following error message ("Database Error - database disk image is malformed"), it’s possible that the local copy of the sync database has become corrupted. To resolve this, you can download the database again.

![](../../d33v4339jhl8k0.cloudfront.net/docs/assets/6081f7f4c9133261f23f4b41/images/676c4abc8edeaf1dd43845f5/file-1tsU3JuPjp.png)

Here’s how to do it step-by-step:

## **Step 1: Back Up Your Notes**

Before proceeding, make sure to back up all your notes. This is crucial to avoid losing any data.

1. Locate where your notes are saved. Refer to the help article: **“**[Where are my notes saved?](31-where-are-my-notes-saved.md)**” **(easiest method is to open NotePlan, and in the top right menu, click on "Show in Finder")

2. Copy the entire folder containing your notes to a safe location, such as your **Documents** folder or your **Desktop**.

![](../../d33v4339jhl8k0.cloudfront.net/inline/124420/321fcbe819e7db6d2d4d883449ae2c4139e065ed/472c6a499f09e75dee51abcbc7d070efb3af7cd9/image.png)

## **Step 2: Close NotePlan and Delete the Local Database**

1. Ensure NotePlan is closed. If it’s already open, quit the app completely.

2. Delete the folder named "co.noteplan.NotePlan3" from your system (we just created a backup of this folder).

## **Step 3: Restart NotePlan**

1. Open NotePlan again.

2. The app will automatically start re-downloading your notes and attachments from the cloud.

**Note:** If you have a large number of notes with many attachments, this process may take several minutes.

## **Step 4: Verify and Troubleshoot**

Once the process is complete, check that all your notes and attachments are restored correctly.
If you encounter any issues, contact support for assistance.
**Important Reminder**
Always ensure you have a backup of your notes before deleting any files. This helps prevent accidental data loss.
